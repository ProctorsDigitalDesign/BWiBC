# Bristol Women in Business Charter Brand Guidelines

## 1. Introduction
The Bristol Women in Business Charter aims to promote gender equality and empower women in the business community of Bristol. These brand guidelines are designed to maintain consistency in visual and written communication across all channels associated with the charter.

## 2. Logo Usage
* The official logo of the Bristol Women in Business Charter is the primary visual identifier.
* Always use the approved logo files provided by the Charter.
* Maintain the integrity of the logo by not altering its proportions, colors, or typography.
* Ensure there is adequate clear space around the logo to maintain visibility and impact.

## 3.1 Color Palette

**Primary Colors (from outer circle of Logo):**
* #ec1e23 (Red)
* #f6911d (Orange)
* #f5e916 (Yellow)
* #19ab49 (Green)
* #1067b0 (Blue)
* #28338f (Dark Blue)
* #612d8f (Purple)
* #c2197c (Magenta)

**Other Text Colour:**
* #444440 (Grey)

## 3.2 Colour choices
Where possible, colours should be chosen based on the type of activity or action;
* **Red** - Action
* **Yellow** - People
* **Purple** - Community - and the main colour of use
* **Green** - celebrating days (e.g International women's day)

## 4. Typography
* **Header Font:** League Spartan (Weight: Bold)
* **Body Font:** Open Sans (Weight: Regular, Italic)

## 5. Tone of Voice
* The tone of voice should be professional, inclusive, and empowering.
* Communicate with clarity and empathy, acknowledging the diverse experiences of women in the business community.
* Use language that encourages participation, collaboration, and positive change.

## 6. Imagery
* Images should reflect diversity and inclusivity within the business community, showcasing women from various backgrounds and industries.
* Avoid stereotypical representations and instead opt for authentic, empowering visuals.
* Wherever possible, use proprietary images rather than stock images.
* Ensure that images are of high quality and resonate with the values of the Bristol Women in Business Charter.

## 7. Social Media Guidelines
* Maintain an active presence on social media platforms to engage with the community and promote events, initiatives, and success stories.
* Respond promptly to messages, comments, and inquiries from followers, fostering a sense of connection and support.
* Use the Charter standard hashtags: `#WiBCharter` `#Bristol` `#WomeninBusiness` `#Equality` `#Diversity` `#Inclusion`

## 8. Brand Consistency
* Ensure consistency in branding across all materials, both online and offline.
* Regularly review and update brand assets to reflect any changes or developments within the Charter.
